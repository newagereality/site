End Times Prophecies • 10 Min Read

Connect the dots between the ahadith.

Disambiguate what we know about the coming of the Mahdi, Isaa, and Dajjal.

The end times ahadith are scattered, and the major signs are not necessarily in order. This article is an attempt to unify them into a coherent chronology.

Please note that the hadith are not necessarily quoted verbatim for easier comprehension.

The Antecedent

Although vague, the following hadeeth implies a buildup to the major events.

"The fitnah of al-ahlas (continuous calamity) is mass desertion and war. Then, the fitnah of as-sarra (meaning 'the rich', when some reach people use their money to hire others to fight for them) will start from under the feet of a man who claims that he is of me (of my descendants). However, he is not of me, for my loyal friends are the ones who have taqwa.

Afterwards, people will unite around a man whose reign is unstable. Then, the fitnah of ad-duhayma (it is called 'dark and black fitnah' because of its enormity) (will start) and will not leave any member of this nation without severely touching him.

When it is thought that its time has come to an end, it will be lengthened. Meanwhile (during this fitnah), a man will wake up as a believer and will meet the night as a disbeliever, until people divide into two camps; a camp of belief that contains no hypocrisy, and a camp of hypocrisy that contains no belief. If this happens, then await the Dajjal on that day or the next."

Ahmad, Abu Dawud and Al-Hakim, Mishkatul-Masabih, vol. 4, no. 5403

These events are unclear but them being fitan imply fighting within the Ummah.

World War 3

The Romans will enter into a peace treaty with you, then you and they will fight together and be victorious; a Christian will say: ‘The Cross has prevailed.’ Then a Muslim will break the Cross. Then the Romans will prove treacherous (breaking the treaty) (and will gather) for the fierce battle.

Ibn Majah 4089

As will later become clear, Persia in this war most likely will be destroyed. The Muslims will be victorious, and the Roman betrayal likely may be their re-conquest of Constantinople. This is in order for the Muslims to eventually re-conquer it in the future.

It makes sense that at this time, the Mahdi will emerge.

There will be a conflict with the death of a caliph.

Abi Dawud 4286

Three will fight one another for your treasure (in Makkah), each the son of a caliph, but none of them will gain it. Then the black banners will come from the east, and they will kill the Arabs. That is the army of the Mahdi.

Ibn Majah 4084

These ahadith imply that a proper Caliphate will return to rule the Hijaz, and I have not speculated on this as there is little to work off of.

In order for the army to march from Persia, the land must be void of authority so that they could possibly march through without having to fight.

The Mahdi will flee to Mecca due to the fitnah of a caliph’s death. Some will pledge allegiance, and then Sufyani’s army from Syria will be dispatched and swallowed by the earth in Hijaz. The saints of Syria and the strong tribes of Iraq will pledge allegiance.

Abi Dawud 4286

This figure from Syria is called the Sufyani, and it is possible that he is aided by the West because it is at this point that 80 banners "will gather for the Malhama." It is possible they will label the Mahdi as the Antichrist to wage their own eschatological war.

World War 4

You will attack Arabia and Allah will enable you to conquer it.

Muslim 2900

The Mahdi with majority support will conquer Arabia, along with his army from Khorasan:

Nothing shall turn them back until their black flags are planted in Jerusalem.

Tirmidhi 2269

The Caliphate will settle in the holy land.

Abi Dawud 2535

This time is called...

The flourishing of Jerusalem.

Abi Dawud 4294

This means that the Zionist regime called Israel will be destroyed. It is likely that at this time, tens of thousands of Jews will seek refuge in Persia as we saw previously that it's regime has fallen.

There will be seventy thousand Jews of Isfahan wearing Persian shawls.

Muslim 2944

However....

Then you would attack Persia and He would make you conquer it.

Muslim 2900

In order for these Jews to remain here until the coming of the Dajjal, they must become dhimmah under the Mahdi. It may be at this point that the Mahdi restores justice as prophecied, because what ensues is near-constant war.

The Mahdi will fill the earth with equity and justice, and he will rule for seven years.

Abi Dawud 4285

The flourishing of Jerusalem will mark the desertion of Madinah, which will mark the start of al-Malhamah.

Abi Dawud 4294

Madinah will be deserted while in its prime.

Riyadh as-Salihin 1823

World War 4 includes the Malhamah, which likely starts with a Roman attack on Madinah.

You will fight Banu Asfar and those who come after you will fight them, until the best of the Muslims go out to fight them, the people of Hijaz.

Ibn Majah 4094

When the fierce battles (malahim) take place, Allah will send a troop of freed slaves who will be the best Arab horsemen and the best armed, with whom Allah will support His religion.

Ibn Majah 4090

The place of assembly of the Muslims at the time of the Malhama will be in al-Ghutah near a city called Damascus, one of the best cities in Syria.

After the Romans take Constantinople, they continue pushing south until they reach North Shaam when the Malhama begins, and the Muslim armies are gathered in Damascus.

And they would fight such a fight the likes of which would not be seen, so much so that even if a bird were to pass their flanks, it would fall down dead before reaching the end of them.

Muslim 2899

The Romans would land at al-A'maq or in Dabiq. An army of the best (soldiers) of the earth will come from ‘the city.’ A third of the army would run away. A third would be martyrs and the third would be conquerors of Constantinople.

Muslim 2897

Although the Arabic text says 'al-Madinah,' it is disputed whether this refers to Madinah al-Munawwarah in Hijaz, or Damascus, which is considered the city.

The Romans in this time demand that those who had been captured from them are returned to them, and the Muslims say that they will never give up on their brothers, which implies that previous to this, the Muslims won against the Romans and captured a huge portion of them, who would go on to convert to Islam.

This army would go to conquer Constantinople.

They will conquer Constantinople with Tasbih and Takbir and will acquire such spoils of war as has never been seen before.

Ibn Majah 4094

Then you would conquer Rome.

Muslim 2900

An army will conquer India and bring their kings in chains.

al-Fitan

This army will return to Shaam to find the Messiah has returned, meaning that they embarked during the reign of the Mahdi and during the appearance of the Dajjal, or towards the end of his free period.

As they distribute the spoils (from Constantinople), Satan would cry: The Dajjal has taken place. When they come to Shaam, Dajjal will come while they are preparing themselves for battle. Then Isaa will descend and lead them.

Muslim 2897

It is possible that the conquest of Constantinople ensued the conquest of the rest of Rome, so that the Dajjal would come soon after.

Fast Forward...

After Yajuj and Majuj have been wiped out, the remaining Muslims use their bows, arrows, shields as firewood for 7 years. Muslims still performing Umrah and Hajj until a man called Dhul Suwaiqatain from Ethiopia demolishes the Kaabah brick by brick, and takes its treasures.

The people will continue performing the Hajj and `Umra to the Ka`ba even after the appearance of Gog and Magog. The Hour will not be established till the Hajj is abandoned.

Sahih al-Bukhari 1593

Leave the Abyssinians alone as long as they leave you alone, for it is only the Abyssinian with short legs who will seek to take out the treasure of the Kaabah.

Mishkat al-Masabih 5429

Dhul Suwaiqatain (literally: One with two lean legs) from Ethiopia will demolish the Kaabah.

Sahih al-Bukhari 1591

Then a man from Qahtan, driving people with his stick, fights the Ethiopians. Isaa at this time will be the Khalifah over the world, and the Messianic reign will ensue until the Final Hour.

The hour will not be established unless a man from the tribe of Qahtan appears, driving the people with his stick.

Sahih al-Bukhari 3517

Ibn Hajar said in Fat'h al-Baari:

Al-Qurtubi said in al-Tadhkirah: His statement, “driving the people with his staff,” is a metaphor for his dominance over them and their submission to him… He said: Perhaps he is Jahjah mentioned in another hadith. The root of Jahjah is shouting, a characteristic that suits the mention of a staff.


I say: This possibility is refuted by the general statement that he is from Qahtan, which implies that he is a freeman, and by the specification of Jahjah that he is a freed slave. Furthermore, it is mentioned that he will be after the Mahdi, following his example, and that he is not inferior to him.

The hadith of al-Qahtani is mentioned immediately after the hadith of Dhul Suwaiqatain destroying the Kaaba by Imam Muslim, possibly alluding to this chronology.
