Yajuj & Majuj and Koka & Vikoka

In Hebrew, they are Gog u-Magog; in Arabic, Yajuj wa-Majuj; and in Sanskrit, Koka and Vikoka.

Both are the final, overwhelming military force that a future promised leader must defeat to inaugurate a new age.

Koka and Vikoka are the supreme generals of the demon Kali (the personification of the Kali Yuga, which could be paralleled with the Dajjal). Yajuj and Majuj are the chaotic forces released toward the end of the world to devastate the earth.

Their appearance marks the absolute bottom of human morality. In the Kalki Purana, their arrival coincides with the total collapse of the Varna system and the rise of cannibalism; Yajuj and Majuj will come after the Malhama which is like a final world war and follows a series of social decadence.

Koka and Vikoka are nearly impossible to kill because they have a certain bond: if one is killed, the gaze or touch of the other immediately brings him back to life. They raise themselves from the dead faster than Kalki can kill them.

Yajuj and Majuj are so numerous that if the first of them drinks from a lake, the last will find it dry. They are both threats that cannot be defeated through standard warfare.

The hero cannot defeat them through sheer strength alone. Kalki only defeats the twins by separating them and striking them simultaneously (as instructed by Brahma); Muslims will be brought to safety while Allah will destroy Yajuj and Majuj with a certain plague.
