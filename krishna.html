Musa and Krishna • 5 Min Read

Similarities Betwen What Was Said About Musa and Krishna.

These indicate that the Indians received prophets who taught them about Musa, or that they received the message of Islam, and distorted what was taught to them.

Their Beginnings

• A ruler (the Pharaoh of Egypt / King Kamsa of Mathura (Misr?)) received a prophecy that a child would be born who would eventually cause his downfall.

• To prevent this, both rulers ordered the slaughter of newborn males.

• To save them, both infants were placed in baskets by their parents and carried on a river.

• Both were raised by foster parents who were powerful authoritative people (Krishna, by Nanda, chieftain of Gokul).

Their Life Missions

• Both left the lands where they were raised (Egypt/Gokul) to return to their people's "true" roots.

• Both figures were personally connected to the tyrants they were destined to overthrow. Moses grew up in the Pharaoh’s household; Kamsa was Krishna’s biological maternal uncle.

• Musa led the Exodus; Krishna led a mass migration of the Yadava clan from Mathura to the coastal city of Dwarka to escape constant attacks.

• Both of them witnessed the internal strife or rebellion of their people (the Israelites' wandering/idolatry; the Yadavas' eventual self-destruction in the Mausala Parva).

Their Miracles

• Krishna subdued Kaliya, a multi-headed serpent (naga) that poisoned the holy river and terrified villagers. Musa brought the villagers to see him subdue the serpent magic.

• Musa famously parted the Red Sea. During Krishna's birth, the Yamuna river is parted or receded to allow his father to carry him to safety. When Krishna led the Yadavas to Dwarka, he asked the sea to recede to provide land for his people to settle.

• Krishna miraculously cured the hunger of his people in exile, like Manna and Salwa.

The Mountain

• Both of them were involved in a mountain being lifted above the heads of their people.

• Krishna lifted the Govardhan Hill on his little finger to protect the villagers and cattle of Vrindavan from the devastating rains sent by Indra, the king of the devas. • In the early Jewish Mekhilta, some traditions say that Allah placed the mountain over the Israelites for protection from the thunder and lightning, rather than as a threat like the Quran clarified.

Their Adversaries

Karna or Korah/Qarun:

• Were both noble and extremely wealthy; Karna’s nickname "Vasu" means wealth or treasure, and "Sena" means army or host, implying a wealth of riches.

• His gold armor (Kavacha) and earrings is like the gold keys of Qarun.

• Karna’s chariot wheel suddenly sank deep into the muddy ground and became immovably stuck. Krishna instructed Arjuna to attack. Qarun was swallowed up by the earth due to his arrogance from his gold.

Others:

• Shishupala, king of the Chedi, accused Krishna of adultery. In some weak narrations, Qarun bribed a woman to accuse Musa of adultery.

• Musa accidentally killed an oppressive government soldier. While entering Mathura to confront Kamsa (the tyrant), Krishna saw the royal washerman carrying Kamsa's clothes. He refused to give Krishna some clothes, being disrespectful and blasphemous. Krishna slapped him, which severed his head.

Other Connections

• Aaron is the elder brother of Moses, and Balarama is the elder brother of Krishna. Both are significant companions and supporters.

• Balarama is revered as the Adi-Guru, the first spiritual master; Aaron was first high priest of Bani Isra'il and patriarch of the priest lineage.
