Ibrahim and Prahlada • 3 Min Read

Similarities Betwen What Was Said About Ibrahim and Prahlada.

These indicate that either the Indians received prophets who taught them about Ibrahim, or that they received the message of Islam and distorted what was taught to them.

Their Beginnings

• Hiranyakashipu, after receiving a boon of near-invulnerability, demanded that the universe worship only him. Nimrod is the first man to claim lordship over the earth (Babylon), demanding worship due to his immense strength (strongest hunter).

• Nimrod was warned by astrologers that a child would be born who would destroy his kingdom and his idols. Hiranyakashipu saw his son Prahlada’s devotion to Vishnu as a direct threat to his sovereignty and the "end" of his demonic lineage.

• Hiranyakashipu conquered the three worlds (Heaven, Earth, and Underworld). Nimrod was "king of the whole world" and tried building the Tower of Babel to "reach the heavens" and wage war against God, similar to Hiranyakashipu’s attempt to surpass the power of the Devas.

• Both were raised by foster parents who were powerful authoritative people (Krishna, by Nanda, chieftain of Gokul).

Devoted Youth

• Both were like 'insider' rebels. Prahlada was the king’s son; Ibrahim was son of Azar, chief idol-maker. Both youths rejected the mandated idolatry of their fathers' houses.

• Both went before the king to account for their faith in their lords alone. When asked who the true lord was, Prahlada pointed to Vishnu, and Abraham pointed to Allah.

They Plotted Against Them

• Ibrahim was thrown into a massive furnace. Hiranyakashipu, Prahlada's father, ordered him to sit in a bonfire with his aunt Holika (who had a fireproof cloak on). Both of these were due to their worship of their lord alone.

• Allah commanded the fire to be cold and peaceful for Ibrahim. He walked out of the flames unscathed. In the Puranas, the fireproof cloak flies off Holika and covers Prahlada. He emerged unharmed, meditating on Vishnu.

His Descendants

The Sacrifice

• Virochana (son of Prahdala): In the Puranas, Vishnu disguised himself as a Brahmin and asked Virochana for his life (or his head) as a gift. Virochana willingly gave his life, viewing the sacrifice as a supreme duty.

• Ibrahim was commanded to sacrifice his son by Allah. His son famously complied willingly.

Mahabali - grandson of Prahlada

• An avatar of Vishnu "tripped" Bali by asking for three steps of land and then growing to encompass the universe. Like Jacob in the Bible, Bali was "subdued" by the Divine touch (Vamana placing his foot on Bali’s head), which resulted in a supreme blessing/status.

• Jacob in the Bible had a vision of a "Ladder to Heaven" with three distinct stages (Earth, Ladder, Heaven). The Vamana legend has Three Steps — the first covering the Earth, the second the Heavens, and the third placed on Bali himself.

• Jacob in the Bible was forced to leave his home and live in Egypt but was promised that his descendants would rule and that he would be "a company of nations."

• Mahabali was banished to the netherworld. However, this was a promotion instead of a punishment; Vishnu became the doorkeeper of his palace, and Bali was promised the position of King of Heaven in the next age.

• Ya'qub is the father of the 12 Tribes. Mahabali is associated with a vast lineage of "hundreds of sons", representing the expansion of the "Asura/Hero" race across the earth.
