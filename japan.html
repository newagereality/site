Arabic Roots in Ancient (Middle) Egyptian

Tracing the phonetics and meanings across civilizations.

Anata (Anta) あなた أنت
You → Anta (You)
Dōbutsu 動物 دابّة
Creature → Dabbah (Creature)
Ishi 意思 شاء
To will → Shaa (To will)
Kotoba 言葉 كتب
Words → Kutub (Books / Written words)
Kuni 国 قَوْم
Country / Land → Qawm (People / Nation)
Sakana 魚 سمكة
Fish → Samaka (Fish)
Sake 酒 سكر / سقى
Alcoholic beverage → Sakar (Intoxicant) | Saqi (Giver of drink)
Sama 様 سماء
Honorific for high status → Samaa (Highness)
Shi 詩 شعر
Poetry → Shi'r (Poetry)
Shimei 指名 سَمّ
Name / Designate → Ism / Samm (Name / To name)
Tori 鳥 طائر
Bird → Tā'ir (Bird)
Umi 海 يم
Sea → Yamm (Sea)
Tenten 〃 ً ٍ ٌ
Voicing marks (double stroke) ↔ Tanween (Doubling of vowels at end of words)
