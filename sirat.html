The Sirat and Persian Chinvat

The Chinvat Bridge ("Bridge of Judgement") in Zoroastrianism is a bridge that separates the world of the living from the dead. Souls cross this bridge on the fourth day after death to be judged based on their deeds.

For the righteous, it becomes a wide, easy path to Paradise. For the wicked, it turns into a razor-thin, vertical blade, causing them to fall into Hell.


It was narrated from Abu Hurairah that the Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said:

‘Death will be brought on the Day of Resurrection and made to stand on the Sirat (the Bridge over Hell). It will be said: “O people of Paradise!” And they will look. Anxious and afraid lest they be brought out of the place they are in. Then it will be said: “O people of Hell!” and they will look, hoping that they will be brought out of the place they are in.

Then it will be said: “Do you know what this is?” They will say: “Yes, this is Death.” Then the command will be given for it to be slaughtered on the Sirat, and it will be said to both groups: “It is eternal wherever you are, and there will never be any death therein.”

Sunan Ibn Majah 4327


Abu Sa’id said, “I have come to know that the bridge over Hell is thinner than a hair and sharper than a sword.”

Ṣaḥīḥ Muslim 183
